Why this app exists

Hi, I'm Leonidas.

I built Neomind for one stubborn, slightly embarrassing reason: I was tired of my affirmations living on a pile of handwritten cards.

For years I knew exactly what I had to do to grow. So I wrote my affirmations out on little cards and carried them around like a tiny, chaotic deck of self-help. It worked. It was also wildly inefficient. Organizing them was a mess, finding the right one took forever, and writing new ones meant starting from a blank card and a blank brain.

I've had my share of low points, more than I'd care to list here. But this was never only about climbing out of holes. I'm ambitious. I have things I want to build, a person I want to become, goals I fully intend to reach. And the same practice carried me through both directions: affirmations and visualization. Done consistently, they healed me when I was down and pushed me forward when I was reaching. The problem was never whether it worked. It was that actually doing it was annoying.

I tried the other apps. I really did. None of them were good enough. Not powerful enough, not flexible enough, not built by someone who actually lives this stuff every day. And this is the last thing you want to settle for, because it stands between you and your own mind. So in September 2025, I stopped waiting for someone else to build the thing I needed, and started building it myself.

Here's my honest strategy, and you can hold me to it: I'm building Neomind for me. If I love it, if it genuinely makes my own practice easier and more powerful, then I trust that other people will feel that too. No focus groups, no committee. Just one guy building the tool he wishes existed, and hoping it changes a few lives along the way. Yours, ideally.

So what do I actually want Neomind to do for you?

I want it to make the inner work feel light. To take the practice that quietly changes everything, and strip away every excuse not to do it. No messy cards. No blank-page paralysis. Just open the app, and the version of you that you're becoming is right there, one swipe away.

I want it to be the calm voice in your pocket on the days the loud ones win. To meet you at your lowest and remind you, gently and stubbornly, who you actually are.

And honestly, I want it to feel like it was made by a real human who cares whether it works for you. Because it was.

So that's the deal. You get a tool built by someone who needed it as badly as you might, refined by someone who uses it every single day, and improved by someone who takes it personally when it falls short. Not a product from a company. A practice from a person, handed to you.

Thanks for being here. Really.

— Leonidas
Founder, and entire staff, of Neomind
